Phase 1
Interaction-Level Scores
four scores per interaction · three feed BEI pipeline · CID is standalone
Customer Friction Score (CFS)
Friction experienced by the customer on this interaction. Five-component weighted score. Written to the Interaction Object; feeds issue-level aggregation (Phase 2), which feeds BEI.
dissatisfaction · repeat_contact · time_effort · escalation · transfer → 0–1
Outcome Quality Score
Was the issue resolved and did the interaction close appropriately? Consumed by Risk Modifiers (outcome discount), by the Issue Object at resolution, and by Agent Achievement.
0.80 × issue_resolution_success + 0.20 × finality_alignment
Finality Alignment Score
Sub-score of Outcome Quality only. Did the closure state match what was required? Rewards appropriate follow-up endings. Does not flow to BEI directly.
contact_resolution_status × issue_type context → 0–1
Customer Intent Difficulty (CID)
Inherent complexity of the customer's issue, independent of handling quality. Does NOT feed BEI or friction aggregation. Exits to Agent Achievement and Intent Experience Matrix only.
risk · complexity · dependency · emotion → 0–1 (standalone)
CFS feeds Phase 2 friction aggregation. Outcome Quality feeds Phase 3 as a discount input. Finality Alignment feeds Outcome Quality only — never directly to BEI. CID exits the BEI pipeline entirely.
↓ cfs_score written to Interaction Object · outcome_quality_score written to Interaction Object
Phase 2
Issue-Level Friction Aggregation
interaction CFS scores aggregated across open issues on the Customer Profile → primary BEI input
Compute issue_friction_total — primary friction input to BEI. Sub-computation of BEI, not an independent apex score. Friction is anchored on issues: CFS accumulates per-issue, then issues sum to the customer-level total.
Contribution by Issue Status
OPEN
Mean CFS across all linked interactions, equal weighting. Stacks when multiple open issues exist — each contributes independently.
RESOLVED
Contributes zero to issue_friction_total. BEI decay floor handles residual exposure — see Phase 5.
ABANDONED
Holds latest_cfs at full weight. Frozen — no new interactions within context window. Remains a friction contributor.
Note False resolution amplifier: each detected false resolution (RESOLVED issue reopened) increases that issue's friction contribution by +10% per occurrence. Repeat friction compounds at both the interaction level (CFS) and the issue level (aggregation weight).
↓ issue_friction_total computed
Phase 3
Risk Modifiers
amplifiers increase exposure weight · discounts reduce it · all values scoped to context_window
Concept CFS measures how much friction the customer experienced. Risk Modifiers measure how much that friction exposes the business. Values expire at the context_window boundary — customers without recent activity reset to neutral defaults.
↑ Amplifiers — increase exposure
escalation_sensitivity — where escalation went. Supervisor 0.33 · Complaints 0.66 · Legal 1.00. Composite weight: 0.15
duration_norm — how long the issue has been open, normalised 0–1 with configurable cap. Composite weight: 0.25
urgency — Low 0.30 · Medium 0.60 · High 0.90. Composite weight: 0.15
promotion_risk_modifier — applied at BEI level when a promotion is active (default 1.25×). Not part of amplifier_composite.
↓ Discounts — reduce exposure
outcome_discount — max 40% reduction. Consumes outcome_quality_score from Phase 1 directly. One construct, scored once, discounted once.
sentiment_discount — max 20% reduction. Fires only when sentiment improved. Derived as positive_sentiment_shift from the start → end sentiment label pair.
Discounts are multiplicative, not additive. Max combined reduction: 52% (0.60 × 0.80). Discounts alone cannot reduce BEI to zero.
amplifier_composite = 0.15 × escalation_sensitivity + 0.25 × duration_norm + 0.15 × urgency
(weights sum to 0.55 intentionally — additive headroom on top of friction, not proportional shares of a whole)
discount_composite = outcome_discount × sentiment_discount
↓ amplifier_composite · discount_composite ready
Phase 4 Optional Module
Churn Intent Probability
expressed conversational intent · not behavioural prediction · third BEI input when active
Compute churn_intent_probability — anchored on churn_outcome base weight (0.0–1.0 lookup across 11-status taxonomy), then adjusted upward by flight_readiness signals extracted by CID.
Signals flight_readiness: competitor_named (+0.05) · language_finality (+0.01 per level above 2, max +0.03) · time_pressure (+0.02). Cap: +0.10 above base weight.
Lifecycle Persists while the churn Issue Object is OPEN. Begins decaying after churn issue resolves (churn_decay_window — lifecycle-driven, TBD). Null when module inactive or no churn intent on any open issue.
When the churn module is active and a churn signal is present, churn_intent_probability becomes the third BEI input. BEI is fully meaningful without it — churn adds precision, not a requirement.
↓ All BEI inputs assembled
Phase 5
Business Exposure Index (BEI)
0–1 · "how exposed is the business to losing this customer's revenue right now?" · persistent on Customer Profile
-- Standard path (churn module off or no churn signal):
friction_with_modifiers = (issue_friction_total + amplifier_composite) × discount_composite
BEI = clamp(friction_with_modifiers, 0, 1)

-- Churn module active AND churn_intent_probability present:
max_signal = max(friction_with_modifiers, churn_intent_probability)
min_signal = min(friction_with_modifiers, churn_intent_probability)
BEI = clamp(max_signal + blend_weight × min_signal, 0, 1)
-- blend_weight default 0.25 · calibration pending

-- Promotion amplification (applied after composition, at Stage 2):
amplified_BEI = clamp(BEI × promotion_risk_modifier, 0, 1)

-- Decay floor (prevents cliff-drop on issue resolution):
bei_floor = floor_rate × bei_at_resolution (floor_rate default 0.15)
-- Decays linearly from bei_floor to 0 over bei_floor_decay_window (= context_window × 2.0)
BEI lives on the Customer Profile Object — persistent, updated after each interaction. bei_previous is snapshotted before processing and stored on the Interaction Object alongside bei_new. bei_delta = bei_previous − bei_new is a first-class reportable field: positive = risk reduced, negative = risk increased.
↓ BEI written to Customer Profile · Financial Attribution begins
Phase 6
Financial Attribution
four-stage chain · anchored on resolved_cltv × BEI · all outputs stored on Interaction Object
Stage 1
Resolved Customer Lifetime Value
resolved_sltv — already computed wherever stream resolution completed (Smart Ingestion Tiers 1–2, or CID Tiers 3–4 if deferred). Already stored on the Interaction Object. Available here.

resolved_cltv — customer-level value anchor for all downstream calculations.
Path A: customer_cltv from CRM metadata — used directly if present.
Path B cascade: customer_metrics → blended_metrics → stream average (resolved_sltv).
cltv_resolution_source and cltv_resolution_path stored for full audit trail.
Stage 2
Resolved Revenue at Risk Customer-Level Apex
revenue_at_risk (internal): resolved_cltv × bei_score. When promotion active: resolved_cltv × amplified_BEI where amplified_BEI = clamp(bei × promotion_risk_modifier, 0, 1).
Exception: churn_outcome = not_retained → revenue_at_risk = 0 (revenue is lost, no longer at risk).

resolved_revenue_at_risk (terminal, surfaces in dashboards): revenue_at_risk − promotion_financial_subtraction if configured, else equals revenue_at_risk.
Written back to Customer Profile after each interaction. Read as resolved_revenue_at_risk_previous at the start of the next interaction's attribution.
Stage 3
Net Commercial Delta (NCD) Interaction-Level Apex
ncd = max(resolved_revenue_at_risk_previous − resolved_revenue_at_risk_new, 0) − cts_value

Only positive gross deltas carry forward. When exposure increases, gross delta = 0 and ncd = 0 − cts_value.

cts_value — handling-mode-aware (set by Smart Ingestion via handling_mode + user_id → team_id → team rate config).
Human: effective_hourly_rate × (handle_time_seconds / 3600) or flat per_interaction rate.
Auto-Agent: bot_cost_per_interaction_{channel} flat rate.
Stage 4 — Sub-Scores
revenue_protected
Always computed. Gross delta component of NCD — what NCD would be before CTS is subtracted.
max(prev − new, 0)
revenue_saved Churn
resolved_cltv when churn_outcome is retained or retained_at_risk. Event metric — fires once per retained churn event.
revenue_lost Churn
resolved_cltv when churn_outcome = not_retained. Also triggers Stage 2 zeroing of revenue_at_risk.
Presentation rule: revenue_protected and revenue_saved are mutually exclusive per interaction at the visualisation layer. Revenue Protected is a Stage 3 gross delta sub-score; Revenue Saved is a churn module event metric. Different types — never summed.
✓ All revenue fields written to Interaction Object. Customer Profile updated: resolved_revenue_at_risk (new state) · cts_total (running total). Outputs available to Performance Management.
↓ Scoring outputs available · Performance Management begins
Phase 7
Performance Management
agent-level outputs · powered by interaction and financial data · feeds indices in Phase 8
Agent Achievement Score
Measures how well the agent handled the interaction relative to its difficulty and outcome. Consumes CID (difficulty baseline), Outcome Quality Score (resolution success), and Sentiment Labels (trajectory multiplier). Core agent performance signal.
cid_score · outcome_quality_score · sentiment_trajectory_multiplier → 0–1
Agent Achievement Efficiency Sub-score
Sub-score of Agent Achievement. Adjusts achievement for cost-efficiency — penalises unnecessarily long or expensive interactions for the outcome delivered. Consumes CTS data and handle time relative to stream norms.
outcome_quality_score / normalised_cts_cost → efficiency-adjusted 0–1
Interaction Quality
Measures the quality of the interaction as experienced by the customer — how well the agent navigated the conversation, handled friction triggers, and brought the interaction to an appropriate close. Not the same as Outcome Quality (which is about whether the issue resolved), nor Agent Achievement (which is about how the agent performed relative to difficulty).
Conversation handling · friction navigation · finality alignment context → 0–1
Agent Commercial Impact
Measures the agent's commercial contribution on this interaction. Directly consumes NCD (net commercial delta after CTS) and revenue event metrics (revenue_saved, revenue_lost). Connects agent performance to the financial outcomes it produced.
ncd · revenue_saved · revenue_lost context → commercial impact index 0–1
Agent Achievement Efficiency is a sub-score of Agent Achievement — it adjusts the achievement score for cost. Interaction Quality and Agent Commercial Impact are independent scores, not sub-scores. All four feed Phase 8 indices.
↓ Performance scores computed · Indices begin
Phase 8
Impact Scores
two composite scores · agent-level and customer-level · both feed the apex CII in Phase 9
Agent Impact Index (AII)
Composite agent-level index. Synthesises three Performance Management outputs: Agent Achievement, Interaction Quality, and Agent Commercial Impact. The single-number answer to "how impactful was this agent on this interaction?"
Powered by Performance Management → 0–1 composite index
Customer Impact Score (CIS)
Measures the cumulative impact of all interactions on this customer relationship within the context window — combining risk trajectory (BEI delta), net commercial outcome (NCD), revenue event history, and friction load (CFS). Built from per-interaction IIS values but reported and governed at the customer level.
BEI delta · NCD · revenue events · CFS aggregated per customer → customer-level 0–1
AII and CIS operate at different scopes: AII is per-interaction, per-agent; CIS is per-customer, accumulating across all interactions within the context window. Both roll into the portfolio-level CII in Phase 9.
↓ AII + CIS computed · Portfolio apex
Phase 9
Customer Impact Index (CII) Apex · Board-Level
all customers · all streams · all risk profiles · governed, revenue-aligned replacement for NPS
CII = aggregation of CIS across all customers,
      weighted by BEI (risk exposure) and resolved_cltv (revenue weight),
      segmented across all streams and risk profiles
-- Composition formula and weights: specification pending
NPS Replacement CII occupies the same board-level reporting slot as NPS but is computed continuously from real interaction data rather than collected via survey at low frequency. A single number that moves in response to actual customer outcomes, with full causal traceability into which streams, issue types, risk profiles, and agent behaviours are driving it.
Traceability Every CII movement is explainable bottom-up: CII shifts because CIS shifted for specific customers, because IIS shifted on specific interactions, because BEI or NCD moved, because of specific issue types, agents, or friction patterns. No survey lag, no sample bias, no black box.
Revenue Weighting Unlike NPS, CII weights each customer by resolved_cltv and their current BEI. A deteriorating high-value customer at high risk moves CII more than a stable low-value customer. The index reflects commercial exposure, not just volume of dissatisfaction.
✓ Pipeline complete. Every metric from agent performance to portfolio health is traceable through the full chain back to the raw interaction transcript and CRM metadata ingested at Phase 0.
